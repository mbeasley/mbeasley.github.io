Below are some of the books I’ve read recently and some brief notes about them to help jog my memory. I try to update this list from time to time, but for a more complete list, you can view my reading list on Goodreads.

The Coaching Habit

by Michael Bungay Stanier

Finished on December 27, 2022

tl/dr; Advice is overrated and not actually as helpful as people think. Use very targeted coaching habits to help individuals solve problems themselves and, in turn, grow from doing so.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice habits for coaching when faced with specific triggers, interactions, and situations - and use these new habits to replace less useful habits.
  • Use the seven coaching questions to start removing yourself as the problem solver and coaching others towards solutions:
    1. The Kickstart Question: What’s on your mind? 
    2. The AWE Question: And what else? 
    3. The Focus Question: What’s the real challenge here for you? 
    4. The Foundation Question: What do you want? 
    5. The Lazy Question: How can I help? 
    6. The Strategic Question: If you’re saying Yes to this, what are you saying No to? 
    7. The Learning Question: What was most useful for you?

The 15 Commitments of Conscious Leadership

by Jim Dethmer et al.

Finished on December 19, 2022

tl/dr; Leaders are more effective when they lead with intention (above the line) versus reaction (below the line). The fifteen commitments are a framework to shift your perspective above the line.

Key Takeaways

  • The 15 commitments build from low-level principles to higher-level principles that govern how we relate to ourselves, our communities, and our work.
  • One of the perspectives that stuck out the most for me was deficit vs abundance or sufficiency thinking.
  • The first step in cultivating any formation of the commitments is building up in-the-moment or near-in-the-moment awareness and mindfulness of your current disposition. 

The Manager’s Path

by Camille Fournier

Finished on October 7, 2022

tl/dr; Pragmatic advise at different levels of leadership and management; less of a thesis and more of a manual.

Key Takeaways

  • Re-visit relevant content for where you’re at in your career periodically; consider the levels adjacent to you as well so you can better understand / remember their perspective.
  • The people components really matter. It’s easy to excuse yourself from prioritizing the people - don’t do that.
  • Prioritization is hard. Spend the time and invoke strategies for prioritization so you don’t spend limited time and energy on the wrong things.
